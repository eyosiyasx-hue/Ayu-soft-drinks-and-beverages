Ethiopian Beers የኢትዮጵያ ቢራ

Thirteen lagers from the country's seven operating breweries. Standard bottle is 330ml; the iconoclastic Bedele Special also comes in 500ml. Prices are retail in Ethiopian Birr (ETB), drawn from hotel menus, deliveraddis.com, helloomarket, and trade press reports.

Soft Drinks ለስላሳ መጠጦች

Two families dominate Ethiopia's bottled carbonates — Coca-Cola (bottled by CCBA Ethiopia, full national reach) and Pepsi (bottled by Moha Soft Drinks Industry S.C., currently disrupted). All figures below in ETB.

⚠️ Update on Pepsi / Moha (Dec 2024 – 2025): Per The Reporter Ethiopia, all eight Moha plants have been halted for over a year because of Ethiopia's foreign-exchange shortage — the company can't import syrup, bottles, crates, or spare parts. Pepsi, 7-Up, and Mirinda stock is therefore sporadic at retail; legacy wholesale price lists still circulate. We show the last published RRP and flag availability.

How to read the cards

Cost

Recommended retail in ETB at bottle / can / crate level. Draft ("jambo" ≈ 400ml) usually runs 5–8 ETB less than the bottled price.

Amount

The single-serve volume — typically 330ml bottles for beer, 300ml glass / 330ml PET / 500ml PET / 1L / 1.5L / 2L for soft drinks.

Count

Standard pack-out: a crate is 24 × 330ml for beer; soft drinks ship in 6-, 12-, 24- or 30-bottle cases depending on format.

Availability

Where and how reliably it sells — nationwide, regional, draft, or currently constrained. Bars, supermarkets, kiosks, restaurants, hotels.

Sources & disclaimers

Pricing collected from Ethiopian hospitality price lists, deliveraddis.com, helloomarket, hellooomarket Telegram, The Reporter Ethiopia, Brauwelt, Addis Fortune, Addis Standard, Wine Searcher, Numbeo, Expatistan, and direct brewery pages. Ethiopia has no centralized public price list — these are indicative retail figures as of late 2025; actual bar and kiosk prices vary by city, channel, and inflation. Alcohol sales restricted to persons 21+.
